AnimalViz

AnimalViz

AnimalViz (formerly known as DoggyVision) is a mobile phone application that simulates animals' vision in real-time on the phone's video feed.

Problem:

Current animal science and biological education curriculum struggle to teach the differences between humans' and animals' senses. Many animals' senses are so different from ours that they are hard to comprehend.

Goal:

Design a tool that enables students to engage in firsthand "perspective-taking" of another animal's senses in real-time. Particularly, design an augmented reality application that simulates different animals' visual senses to give students a better understanding of our biological differences.

Research Gathering: Conducting a focus group with families to investigate their scientific curiosities about their pets' senses

In 2018, my collaborators and I led a focus group to investigate what curiosities and questions families, particularly adolescents, have about their pets' senses and experiences. We facilitated discussion among the group with questions we designed ahead of time, as well as a post-it based activity where families could write down responses and questions to prompts we wrote on the board.

Our analysis of the focus group showed us that families were particularly interested in how their pets see and hear the world. Several participants mentioned the desire to use technology to view the world from their pet's perspective. In particular, participants wanted to use this technology to answer questions about how their pets see and feel about the world.

Prototype:

Based on the insight we gained of our user population, we decided to develop an application called DoggyVision, an augmented reality app that helps people see the world with the visual perception of a dog. DoggyVision could be used as a handheld device, and also with Google Cardboard to become an Augmented Reality headmounted display (HMD).

Evaluation: Using DoggyVision with families to spur scientific inquiry and discovery

We structured our evaluation as a three hour workshop with families at a museum on a college campus. Five families participated (8 adults, 9 kids). We opened the workshop with a group discussion about what questions families had about their dogs' visual senses. We then sent each family out on a DoggyVision scavenger hunt where they took photographs of different scenes indoors and outdoors. Afterwards, we facilitated a group sticky-note based discussion about what they observed and other questions they had about their pets (or other animals).

We found DoggyVision to be an effective means in engaging families to scientifically think about the differences in theirs and their pets senses. The families were curious about other species of pets (and non-pets) such as fish, cats, birds, reptiles, and more.

Design revisions and next steps: From DoggyVision to AnimalViz

I am currently working on new designs for AnimalViz which will offer firsthand perspective-taking of vision like DoggyVision, but offer more species' senses to experience. In addition, AnimalViz will simplify the process of saving images and videos. We also received feedback from teachers that a data collection and visualization tool would be nice to have in AnimalViz for classroom work, and I am incoprating this into the designs as well.
